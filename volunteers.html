Thank you for volunteering your time. This portal will help you get up to speed on the basics of audio support for our church services and events.

Getting Started

Follow these steps to get oriented as a new audio volunteer.

1

Review the Training Materials

Head over to the Training tab and go through each module. Focus on the Basics section first — it covers everything you need for your first session.

2

Shadow an Experienced Engineer

Your first 1–2 sessions will be shadowing. Watch how the team sets up, runs soundcheck, and manages the mix during service.

3

Complete the Pre-Service Checklist

Before your first solo session, make sure you can complete every item on the Checklist tab. Ask questions if anything is unclear!

4

Run Your First Session

You'll handle a smaller event or assist during a main service. A team lead will be nearby for support.

Pre-Service Checklist

Use this checklist before each session to make sure everything is ready to go.

Setup (30 min before)

Power on the mixing console and speakersCheck that all power strips are on and LEDs are lit
Set up microphones on stageRefer to the stage plot for placement
Check all cable connectionsXLR cables should click firmly into place
Verify monitor mixes are at reasonable levelsAsk worship team if they can hear themselves

Soundcheck

Line check each mic one at a timeHave someone speak/play into each mic while you verify signal
Set gain levels (green, not red)Adjust preamp gain so the signal peaks in the green zone
Listen for feedback or buzzSlowly bring up faders and listen for unwanted noise
Run through one song with the bandConfirm overall mix balance and stage monitor levels

Post-Service

Mute all channels and lower master faderDo this before powering down to avoid pops
Power off speakers first, then consoleAlways: speakers off first, on last
Coil cables and return mics to storageOver-under coil technique to preserve cable life

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from new audio volunteers.

Do I need prior audio experience?▼
Not at all! We'll train you from scratch. As long as you're willing to learn and show up, we'll get you up to speed.
How often will I need to serve?▼
Volunteers typically serve 1–2 times per month, depending on the schedule and your availability. We'll work around your calendar.
What if something goes wrong during service?▼
Stay calm — it happens to everyone. Check the Troubleshooting guide in Training. If you can't fix it quickly, a team lead is always a text away.
What's the difference between a volunteer and an engineer?▼
Volunteers help with setup, teardown, and basic mixing for smaller events. Engineers handle the full mix for main Sunday services. Many engineers started as volunteers!
Can I eventually become a full audio engineer?▼
Absolutely! Once you're comfortable with the basics and have served several sessions, talk to Steven about joining the engineering team.
When should I arrive before service?▼
Plan to arrive at least 30 minutes before the service starts. This gives time for setup and soundcheck.
